ABC Call Volume Trend Analysis: Optimizing Call Center Efficiency
# 📌 Project Overview
This project, developed as part of the Trainity Internship, delivers an in-depth Call Volume Trend Analysis for the ABC Call Center.
The primary business problem addressed:

Manpower planning
Reducing an excessively high call abandonment rate
Using structured data analysis & visualization, I identified key demand patterns, quantified staffing gaps, and built a data-backed manpower strategy to reduce the abandonment rate to a maximum of 10%.
The recommendations provide management with an actionable roadmap to improve service quality, resource allocation, and customer experience.

# 🎯 Impact & Key Results
This analysis translates complex operational data into business intelligence with measurable outcomes:

Metric	Business Insight	Actionable Result	Quantified Impact
Call Volume Peak	Highest volume between 10 AM – 2 PM	Identified critical staffing shortfall	Recommended shift adjustment → reduced 54% peak abandonment rate to target 10%
Data Handled	Processed 117,990 rows × 13 columns	Cleaned, validated, and analyzed large operational dataset	Handled 47,877 abnormal/null values
Call Duration Peak	Avg. call duration peaks at 203s (4–9 PM) vs. 199s overall	Identified higher complexity calls	Suggested allocation of senior/specialized agents in evening
Night Shift Planning	Baseline demand = 30 calls/night per 100 daytime calls	Built 24/7 manpower model	Ensured SLA compliance across all hours

# 🔍 Strategic Recommendations
Immediate Shift Adjustment

Reallocate staff to 10 AM – 2 PM block (critical shortfall).
Evening Shift Specialization

Assign experienced agents (4–9 PM) to handle longer/complex calls (avg. 203s).
Night Shift Optimization

Adopt calculated night shift manpower plan (based on baseline demand factor).
